# LEV Gijón 2026 — A Listening Guide

A pre-festival listening companion for [LEV Festival](https://levfestival.com/) Gijón, **30 April – 3 May 2026** (20th edition). Built as a journey across the four venues and the four movements of an LEV night: **Approach → Texture → Body → Descent.**

[**Open the playlist on Spotify →**](https://open.spotify.com/playlist/1e9OemZ6VuCm61fpRFyv0s)

LEV (Laboratorio de Electrónica Visual) is an experimental electronic + audiovisual festival across four venues:

- **Teatro de la Laboral** — concert hall, drone and contemplative work
- **Muséu del Pueblu d'Asturies** — daytime sets in an ethnographic museum
- **LA NAVE** — late-night club, deconstructed body music
- **Jardín Botánico** — Sunday closing in a botanical garden

The playlist mirrors that arc. Listen end-to-end before you arrive; it sets the ear.

---

## Movement I — Approach

*Slow drone. The body settles. The room becomes audible.*

### 1. Abul Mogard — *Above All Dreams*

[Listen](https://open.spotify.com/track/6Re4HgAPT39gOc8a0ioB2d) · 2018, Ecstatic

The Serbian retired metalworker's title track from his *Above All Dreams* LP. Synth pads built from modulated factory recordings — the sound of slow rust. Mogard plays Teatro de la Laboral; this is the language of that room.

### 2. Alva Noto — *Xerrox Voyage* (from *Xerrox, Vol. 4*)

[Listen](https://open.spotify.com/track/5EHIsiLxh7ZPe8QCR90m6K) · 2020, Noton

Carsten Nicolai's fourth and longest *Xerrox* installment — copies of copies of copies, decayed and beatific. *Voyage* is the album's entrance: a slow ascent. Geometry of static.

### 3. Ryoichi Kurokawa — *Sea In You*

[Listen](https://open.spotify.com/track/2l7oy3hzqGLfErfq9bqDwo)

Japanese audiovisual composer — granular field recordings layered into architectural drift. Kurokawa's work always exists in dialogue with image; on record alone, the visual is implied as absence. Pay attention to the depth of the stereo field.

### 4. Sara Muñiz — *Animus*

[Listen](https://open.spotify.com/track/4HSl5ZSwcwUIPLBCHiA0qo)

Asturian composer working in slow-burn drone and modular synthesis. *Animus* (the title track of her 2023 LP) sits between liturgy and signal. A local voice for a local festival.

## Movement II — Texture

*Pulse begins. Rhythm without dancefloor commitment yet — the body listens forward.*

### 5. NAH (Michael Kuhn)

*Not on Spotify.* NAH's catalogue (Software, Where To Now?) is Bandcamp / vinyl only. Slot intentionally left open — substitute from Bandcamp during your own listen, or skip. His material sits between this µ-Ziq cut and what comes after.

### 6. µ-Ziq — *Brace Yourself Jason* (from *Lunatic Harness*)

[Listen](https://open.spotify.com/track/4Z8jptUDjJDuxBf0pSOvSi) · 1997, Planet Mu

Mike Paradinas at his most melancholic — not the breakcore µ-Ziq, the elegiac one. Picked deliberately over *Hasty Boom Alert*: this is the version of µ-Ziq that pairs with ID:Mora at LEV.

### 7. Julek Ploski — *Horses*

[Listen](https://open.spotify.com/track/1dOAP5tA79nmyqsMrXYaSo)

Polish producer in the deconstructed club / patient techno lineage. *Horses* gallops without urgency. Listen to how the kick implies a pulse it never resolves.

### 8. Tensal — *Dextra* (from *Renovatio*)

[Listen](https://open.spotify.com/track/1sDyQQP0unQifKQqBkXkNu)

Spanish techno producer — clean, propulsive, monastic. *Dextra* is one of the most uncompromising tracks here. The pulse the previous tracks were avoiding finally arrives.

## Movement III — Body

*Peak club. LA NAVE at 3am. Deconstructed but committed.*

### 9. Komatssu — *Akabane* (from *Ataxia*)

[Listen](https://open.spotify.com/track/6xkjFB6X6qnqAfKAgyx8wF)

Japanese-trained, Berlin-rooted producer. *Akabane* (named after the Tokyo neighbourhood) is industrial techno with a forensic ear for negative space.

### 10. Catnapp — *Damage*

[Listen](https://open.spotify.com/track/5hiiuq5cNjFzhDKlVR9rJA) · 2020, Monkeytown

Argentinian-Berlin producer Amparo Battaglia. *Damage* is the title track of her most direct LP — vocoded vocals over a kick that refuses to apologise.

### 11. Jlin — *Borealis* (with Björk, from *Akoma*)

[Listen](https://open.spotify.com/track/6YPfJUjeeJM1S5IrrgJi9Y) · 2024, Planet Mu

Jerrilynn Patton's fourth album, and arguably her most generous. *Borealis* features Björk; the rhythm is footwork-derived but expanded into something orchestral. Patient and ferocious at once.

### 12. Meuko! Meuko! — *Ghost Island* (from *鬼島 / Ghost Island*)

[Listen](https://open.spotify.com/track/5tTjd6wLTqQioGRAyv7pXL) · 2018, Nervous Horizon

Taipei producer Hsiangfu Chen. *Ghost Island* the title track of the 2018 album (likely the same release referenced as *11 F* on the LEV bill). Mandopop fragments shredded through club-construction logic. The most disorienting track here in the best way.

### 13. Corin — *Lux Aeterna*

[Listen](https://open.spotify.com/track/1RBtuH2Wv2vTRhDMGpAeMh) · 2020, Sumac

Australian producer Corin Ileto. *Lux Aeterna* (eternal light) — Catholic liturgy as deconstructed club. The peak. Whatever happens after this is descent.

## Movement IV — Descent

*Ambient close. Sunday at Jardín Botánico. The body reassembles.*

### 14. Nova Materia — *Aparece en Sueños*

[Listen](https://open.spotify.com/track/7xeaeDjIrIq9aGX5nDARYw)

French-Chilean duo Caroline Chaspoul & Eduardo Henríquez (ex-Pánico). Their percussive material (*Xpujil*) is what they're known for; this is the contemplative side. *Appears in Dreams* — slow modular textures, no kick.

### 15. NikNak — *Mere Data* (with SlowPitchSound, from *Ireti*)

[Listen](https://open.spotify.com/track/6BAV3pm4qoQ7HZU0tAi58s) · 2024

UK turntablist Nicole Raymond. *Bashful* (her 2022 album referenced on the LEV bill) is not on Spotify; *Mere Data* from *Ireti* is the closest analogue — layered turntable manipulation, ambient and architectural rather than club.

### 16. Musgö — *Rezo de protección* (from *La grieta*)

[Listen](https://open.spotify.com/track/1Q0zTPjazsJctJrc9x4EoC)

Spanish project — flamenco fragments, processed voice, synth drones. *Prayer of Protection* — exactly what the descent requires.

### 17. Abul Mogard — *In a studded procession* (from *Quiet Pieces*)

[Listen](https://open.spotify.com/track/6f8oPer4BSAmcGeWJJI1sp)

We close where we began, but quieter. *Quiet Pieces* is Mogard at his most withdrawn — barely audible synth motion, the sound of light through dust. The festival has happened. The room is empty. You walk out.

## Notes & caveats

- **NAH (Michael Kuhn)** is on the LEV bill but has no Spotify catalogue under that name. The slot is intentional — fill it from Bandcamp if you want.
- **Sara Muñiz** identity: there is exactly one Sara Muñiz on Spotify with an album titled *Animus*. Plausibly the LEV composer (her catalogue includes a film score, *Retaguardia*, suggesting she scores), but I cannot independently verify against the festival programme.
- **Meuko! Meuko! *11 F*** does not appear on Spotify; *Ghost Island* (2018) is the closest match and is likely the same release under a different title.
- **NikNak *Bashful*** is not on Spotify. Substituted with *Mere Data* from *Ireti* (2024), her newer album in the same idiom.
- Other artists on the LEV bill (**Cod.Act, Tacit Group, NonTech, ID:Mora, Enrique del Castillo, Myriam Bleau & Pierce Warnecke, Hsiangfu Chen** as a solo artist) are not on Spotify. Their work lives at the festival itself.
