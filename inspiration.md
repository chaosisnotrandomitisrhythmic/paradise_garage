# Inspiration

## Paradise Garage

The Paradise Garage (1977–1987) in New York City was the temple of dance music. DJ Larry Levan mixed on a custom Richard Long sound system with Bozak rotary mixers and massive Altec Lansing Voice of the Theatre speaker cabinets. The system was designed for fidelity — not volume — making every record sound like a live performance on the dancefloor.

The philosophy: **the sound system is the instrument, the DJ is the musician.**

## Barco Sound House — Madrid

[Barco Sound House](https://www.bsoundhouse.com/) (C/ del Barco 34, Malasaña) carries the Paradise Garage lineage into Madrid.

**Sound system:**
- [OJAS](https://ojas.nyc/) / [NNNN](https://nnnn.no/) custom speakers
- [Admire Audio](https://admireaudio.com/) VOTT A5 (inspired by Altec Voice of the Theatre)
- McIntosh amplification
- Altec Lansing components

**Vibe:** Vinyl-first programming, curated selectors, 70s/80s analog disco aesthetic. Open Wed–Sun, 6PM–6AM.

The whole signal chain — vinyl → tube amplification → horn-loaded speakers — is the same DNA as the Paradise Garage, 45 years later.
