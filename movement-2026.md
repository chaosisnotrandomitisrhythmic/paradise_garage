# Movement Detroit 2026 — Ellen Allien b2b DJ Stingray 313

A listening guide built around a single set: **Ellen Allien back-to-back with DJ Stingray 313**, Underground Stage, Hart Plaza — **Saturday 23 May 2026, 10:30pm–midnight**, at the 20th-anniversary edition of [Movement](https://www.movement.us/).

No streaming link on this one — by design. These records live on [Bandcamp](https://bandcamp.com/), on [Discogs](https://www.discogs.com/), on vinyl. Digging for them *is* the exercise. Where a track has a home you can buy it from, it's linked; otherwise the catalogue reference (label, year) is the map.

Two cities, two opposite ways of holding a room:

- **DJ Stingray 313** — Detroit. Electro: fast, surgical, broken-beat, forward-facing.
- **Ellen Allien** — Berlin. Techno: hypnotic, emotional, four-to-the-floor, patient.

The whole week was the city's first official **Detroit Techno Week**: Dopplereffekt live, a Tresor takeover with Surgeon and Underground Resistance, DVS1's Wall of Sound at Bert's Warehouse. The B2B sat at the centre of it — Berlin paying respects to the city that invented the music.

---

## How the two of them play

### DJ Stingray 313 — the surgical side

- **~100+ tracks in a two-hour set**, dual 1210s + CDJs. Quick chops, flash mixes, sudden switches — collage, not long blends.
- Starts on rolling four-to-the-floor, then **strips the rhythmic anchors** so the set turns "dirtier, darker and more twisted."
- The method, in his words: *"I gotta make people comfortable first. Then when they're ready, I drop things on them."* Tension and release as structure, not garnish.
- Lineage: Drexciya's touring DJ (named "Stingray" by James Stinson), taught to DJ by **Moodymann**, raised on the Electrifying Mojo's radio. The SWAT mask is a focus trigger — *"when this goes on, it's all business."*
- Creed: *"We don't need records that sound like they were made 30 years ago."* Electro as forward sound design, not nostalgia.

### Ellen Allien — the hypnotic side

- Berlin. Founder of **BPitch** and the rawer **UFO Inc.**
- Driving, emotional, hypnotic techno, often with her own spoken word arriving "like signals from another planet."
- **Longer builds** against Stingray's collage — she holds a groove and lets it breathe where he cuts.
- New album *New Life* — "emotional rave music," themes of empowerment and collective action — out **9 July 2026**.

The real question in a B2B like this: **how do broken electro beats and straight four-to-the-floor techno reconcile in real time?** That bridge is the whole lesson — and the reason this pairing matters for the build.

## The crate — sequenced as a set arc

A reconstruction of the territory the set moves through, not the literal setlist (no community tracklist exists yet — see caveats). Sourced from Stingray's own published selections and the two artists' catalogues.

### I. The opener + the engine — DJ Stingray 313

Where he starts and the productions that define his own sound.

- **Arpanet — *Ionic Crystals*** · [Aqualung] — *his stated starter track.* "So futuristic, with beautiful string work and modulated synth loops." (Arpanet = Gerald Donald, the other half of Drexciya — so the set opens inside the lineage.)
- **DJ Stingray 313 — *Silicon Romance* / *Serotonin* / *Straight Up Cyborg* / *Wire Act*** · from *Aqua Team* (Micron Audio) — the home productions. Cold, fast, funk-underneath-the-chrome electro. Dig the whole *Aqua Team* LP on [his Bandcamp](https://djstingray313.bandcamp.com/).

### II. The selections — what he reaches for

Pulled from his own published crate notes. Each line is his.

- **soft grunge — *Bass In Your Chest*** · [Posse Up!] — "Brings the funk to you without pause."
- **Moogie Fox & Philo — *Bouge Ton Cul*** · [Nice & Deadly] — "A really nice intro that lets me get out of any track." *(his escape-hatch tool — note how a DJ keeps a reliable exit in the bag.)*
- **Dagga — *Technobass Freak*** · [Bogoture] — "Funky synths, immaculate drum programming and cyborg vocals."
- **Yazzus — *ONHCET*** · [Juicy Gang] — "Dystopian, high-speed tech/electro. Solid kick."
- **NN — *Deception*** · [47] — "Refined industrial in an electro package. Cinematic sound design." *(vinyl/Bandcamp only.)*
- **HomeSick — *1800AREYOUSLAPPIN* (Sinistarr Remix)** · [Worst Behavior] — "Intricate sampling and drum work. Heavy bass." *(Bandcamp only.)*
- **Alignment — *Devil Dance*** · [DUEL] — "Hard-hitting, refined, industrial waveforms."
- **Robert Hood — *Ignite A War*** · [Rekids] — "Hypnotic from start to finish, with a no-nonsense bassline." *(the four-to-the-floor pivot — Hood is where electro hands off to techno.)*

### III. The counterweight — Ellen Allien

The 4/4 pole. Where Stingray cuts, she sustains.

- **Ellen Allien — *Innocence*** · from *Nost* (BPitch) — hypnotic, melancholic, built to ride for minutes.
- **Ellen Allien — *UFO*** — the UFO Inc. aesthetic in one track: raw, propulsive, a little unhinged.
- **Ellen Allien — *Steh Auf*** — the political, high-energy side; techno as a stance.
- **Ellen Allien — *Stadtkind*** — the BPitch classic, the Berlin-city-child template. Her whole catalogue is on [her Bandcamp](https://ellenallien.bandcamp.com/).

### IV. The lineage — Detroit electro canon

Where all of it comes from. Not necessarily in the set, but in the room's DNA — and the week's.

- **Cybotron — *Clear*** · 1983 [Fantasy] — Juan Atkins & Rik Davis. Ground zero. Electro-funk that techno grew out of.
- **Drexciya — *Wavejumper*** — the aquatic-electro mythology; James Stinson & Gerald Donald, Stingray's mentors. Reissues via [Clone](https://clone.nl/).
- **Dopplereffekt — *Pornoactress*** · from *Gesamtkunstwerk* — Gerald Donald's clinical, sci-fi electro. Played **live** at Movement this year.

## The move to steal for the build

The whole craft lesson sits at the **seam between section II and III** — the handoff from Robert Hood's *Ignite A War* into Ellen Allien's hypnotic 4/4. That is the **electro-broken-beat → four-to-the-floor transition**, the exact problem a B2B like this has to solve live: reconciling two rhythmic grammars without the floor noticing the join.

For our rig (two laptops + Kaossilator + Xone:96 + turntables), that's the playable territory — using the Kaossilator's looper to carry a pulse across the change while one deck switches grammar underneath. "Play the room, not the records," the same ethos as the [Inspiration](inspiration) page: become the bridge between the two feels rather than just crossfading between them.

The other lesson is structural and pure Stingray: **comfortable foundation first → strip the anchors → drop the strange material once the floor is locked in.** Tension architecture, not just track selection.
